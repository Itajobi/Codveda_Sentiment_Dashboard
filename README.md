# Key Insights
## Overall Engagement
The dataset contains 732 social media posts, generating over 31K likes and 16K retweets, indicating high user engagement.
## Platform Performance
Instagram records the highest number of posts and likes, suggesting stronger user interaction compared to Twitter and Facebook.
Facebook shows relatively lower engagement despite a sizable number of posts.
## Sentiment Trends
Positive sentiment dominates the dataset, followed by emotions such as Joy and Excitement.
Negative sentiments appear less frequently, indicating generally favorable user expression.
## Temporal Patterns
Social media activity shows a noticeable increase over time, with a sharp rise in recent years.
Monthly analysis reveals fluctuations, suggesting seasonality in posting behavior.
## Geographical Distribution
Aggregations such as post count and engagement metrics were computed dynamically within Power BI visuals using built-in grouping and summarization.
The United States records the highest number of social media posts (188), followed by the United Kingdom (143) and Canada (135), as indicated by the largest bubbles on the map.
## Engagement by Platform
Likes distribution shows that Instagram contributes the largest share of total likes, reinforcing its role as a high-engagement platform.
